## Design Context

### Users
Hackathon demo operators and Voodoo/Anthropic judges watching a live pipeline turn gameplay videos into playable ads. Users need to understand progress, spot missing assets, intervene quickly, and trust that the system can recover when generation is imperfect.

### Brand Personality
Confident, operational, and sharp. The interface should feel like a production control room for creative automation: calm under pressure, precise about pipeline state, and polished enough for a jury demo.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light, utilitarian, and focused. Keep the existing white/soft-blue app language, restrained borders, compact cards, and high-contrast black/blue actions. Avoid marketing-style landing pages, generic dark dashboards, excessive gradients, and decorative visuals that compete with asset previews.

### Design Principles
- Show the real pipeline state before flourish.
- Keep asset decisions scannable: coverage, status, preview, prompt, and next action should be visible without hunting.
- Treat AI uncertainty as inspectable data through prompts, style locks, errors, and missing-asset coverage.
- Preserve a modular boundary between UI orchestration and the underlying asset pipeline.
- Optimize for live-demo confidence: fast feedback, clear fallback states, and no hidden critical actions.
